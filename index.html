How it works

Web3 Staking in three steps

Start with a wallet, choose a supported asset, and sign only after the app shows the live route, network, timing, and cost context.

Step 01

Connect your wallet

Open the dashboard and connect your own Web3 wallet. The app reads eligible balances without taking custody of your keys.

Step 02

Pick asset and network

Choose a supported staking asset such as Ethereum staking or SOL where available, then review the network and protocol terms.

Step 03

Confirm and track

Sign the transaction from your wallet, monitor the position in the app, and follow the stated unstaking flow when you are ready to exit.

FAQ

Web3 Staking questions

01

What is Web3 Staking?

Web3 Staking is a non-custodial DeFi app for staking supported crypto assets from your wallet while reviewing live reward, timing, cost, and exit details before signing.

02

Which assets can I stake?

The app is built for supported assets and chains, including ETH, SOL, USDT-based opportunities, and other DeFi staking routes when they are available in the dashboard.

03

How long does staking take?

Timing depends on the selected network, wallet confirmation, validator or protocol processing, and any unstaking rules. The app shows the current status for your route.

04

What does Web3 staking cost?

You may pay network gas, validator or protocol costs, and any route-specific fees shown in the app. Check live Ethereum gas conditions before confirming.

05

Is Web3 Staking safe?

Web3 Staking is non-custodial, but staking still carries smart contract, validator, liquidity, slashing, oracle, and market risks. Read each prompt before you sign.

06

Which chains are supported?

Supported chains vary by asset, wallet, and protocol availability. See the full list on the supported chains page.
